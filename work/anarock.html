Project overview

A unified platform for listings, CRM, and commissions

Anarock is a B2B platform for channel partners (brokers) to browse property listings, share projects with clients, manage leads through an in-app CRM, and track post-sale earnings through commission management. The goal was to replace fragmented, offline workflows with one credible, end-to-end mobile experience.

Project type

End-to-end app + branding

Industry

Real Estate Technology (PropTech)

Tools

Figma, FigJam, ClickUp, Google Meet

Duration

6 months

Role

Led design across modules and user levels

I led the end-to-end product design across key modules, translating complex broker workflows into an intuitive mobile experience that supports both individual channel partners and organizations.

Responsibilities

  • UX research, workflow mapping, and information architecture across listings, CRM, and finance.
  • Wireframing and interaction design for onboarding, verification, browsing, and sharing flows.
  • Visual design and branding: typography, iconography, and motion guidelines where needed.
  • Component consistency for mobile UI to keep screens scalable across modules.
  • Project collaboration and handoff with product and engineering teams.

Problem

Broker workflows were fragmented, offline, and hard to trust

Channel partners rely on speed and personal networks, but their work is often managed across scattered tools and offline methods. That fragmentation reduces efficiency, causes lead leakage, and creates inconsistent client communication.

  • Listings, lead tracking, and commission follow-ups were managed across separate tools.
  • Manual workflows caused missed opportunities and reduced professional credibility.
  • No single platform supported the full CP journey from onboarding to post-sale finance tracking.
  • Clients hesitated to trust brokers due to unverified credentials and inconsistent communication.

Goals

Align user needs with trust and scalability

The product needed to solve daily broker tasks while supporting Anarock’s goal of building a verified, scalable channel partner ecosystem.

User needs

  • Simple onboarding for individuals and organizations, including verification (e.g., RERA).
  • A trusted identity artifact (Digital Visiting Card) to build credibility with clients.
  • Fast browsing, filtering, and sharing of property listings.
  • A centralized space to manage leads, track progress, and monitor commissions.

Business goals

  • Digitize broker operations to reduce lead leakage and improve sales efficiency.
  • Promote verified CPs to strengthen trust and brand credibility.
  • Increase engagement through incentives like early commission offers.
  • Support both individual and organizational users within one platform.

Solution

A mobile-first system that connects discovery, selling, and earnings

The final product delivers a streamlined platform for channel partners to work end-to-end: onboard and verify, discover and share listings, manage clients through a CRM, and track earnings through commission management.

Key highlights

  • Onboarding for individuals and organizations with verification steps.
  • Digital Visiting Card to help CPs present verified identity to clients.
  • Project listing with filters, sorting, project details, and sharing.
  • In-app CRM for client lifecycle and pipeline visibility.
  • Commission management to track earnings and early commission offers.
  • Organization-only “My Team” module with role-based management.

Design focus

Trust signals and role-aware UX were non-negotiable

Verification built into onboarding

Onboarding captures identity and organization details early, so verified profiles and trust indicators can be surfaced consistently across the product.

RBAC-like separation for organization accounts

Organization-only capabilities (like My Team) remain hidden for individual CPs. Owners and admins can manage members and roles, while the rest of the app stays consistent across account types.

Keep high-value tasks shallow

Listing discovery, sharing, and client updates were designed to be fast and repeatable, prioritizing scan-ability and quick actions over heavy form input.

Final flows

Core workflows and screens

The screens below show how the experience supports the complete CP journey, from onboarding and trust-building to selling and post-sale tracking.

Onboarding (CP / Organization)

Phone-based onboarding guides users through profile setup and account type selection, with organization and RERA details captured where applicable.

Digital Visiting Card

A shareable identity artifact that helps CPs communicate verified credentials and improve client trust. Users can create a new card from details, select a template, or upload an existing card.

Project discovery and listing

CPs can search, filter, and sort projects, then move into project details and share information with clients quickly.

Project details and sharing

Project details are structured for quick scanning, with key actions (favorite, share, compare) accessible without burying primary content.

Homepage and navigation

The homepage combines nudges, banners, CRM stage summaries, and finance snapshots so CPs can act quickly without hunting through modules.

Profile

Profile management supports frequent updates (photo, contact details, and verification) without forcing users through heavy re-onboarding.

My Team (organization accounts only)

The My Team module is visible only for organization profiles. Owners and admins can add members, edit roles, and manage team structure.

Outcome

A more professional, credible experience for brokers and clients

The application supports a complete broker workflow while reinforcing trust through verification and consistent communication artifacts (like the Digital Visiting Card). Organization accounts gain team management without fragmenting the core experience for individual CPs.

Reflection

Designing for trust was as important as designing for speed

The core challenge was supporting a diverse user base, from independent brokers to organizational teams, while keeping the experience lightweight. Verified profiles, a shareable Digital Visiting Card, and account-type based access helped professionalize broker workflows without adding unnecessary complexity.

Next

Strengthen collaboration features without bloating the core app

  • Expand organization controls (roles, permissions, and audits) as team usage scales.
  • Improve commission visibility with clearer status explanations and next-step guidance.
  • Refine listing sharing with client-specific summaries (without duplicating work for CPs).
