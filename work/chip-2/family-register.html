MODULE · CHIP 2.0

Geo-scoped Family Register with DHS capture gates

A field-first register that lets frontline workers discover houses and families by area, keep family + member profiles updated, and run DHS-style capture flows with “presence”, GPS, and photo gates. The UI is built to handle daily realities: incomplete data, updates required, duplicates, and interruptions.

Problem

Registers break when discovery, identity, and survey gates are disconnected

Family data is not collected in a clean office flow. Workers operate in a geography, move house-to-house, and update records over time. If discovery (where am I?), identity (who is this?), and survey gates (am I present at the house, do I have GPS/photo?) are split across screens or hidden behind admin logic, field teams either skip steps or create messy duplicates. This module keeps “what needs to happen next” visible as a state.

What the Family Register + DHS module must solve

  • Geo-first discovery: filter by state/district/block/PHC/SHC/village/area and land on the right households quickly.
  • House → family structure: multiple families per house and multiple members per family must be legible.
  • State-based work: “Survey pending” and “Update required” must drive the next action.
  • DHS gates: explicit prompts for presence, house location, and house photo before survey capture.
  • Safe edits: member delete, “member not alive”, and key identity changes require confirmations and clear consequences.

Users

Same register, different accountability

This module is used in the field, but it impacts the entire system. Frontline roles need speed and clarity, supervisors need correction paths, and the platform needs predictable states to avoid garbage-in reporting.

ASHA / Field worker

Discovers households by area, updates family profile fields, adds members, and triggers surveys when present at the beneficiary’s house.

ANM / CHO

Works on larger survey coverage, handles updates required, and ensures house location/photo and identity fields are captured reliably.

Supervisor / Program admin

Resolves data quality issues: duplicates, incomplete identities, and corrections that would otherwise cascade into reporting and follow-up failures.

Facility users

Need family/member profiles to be trustworthy for downstream services, referrals, and continuity workflows that depend on correct household context.

Flow

House → family → member, with DHS gates before capture

The primary navigation is not “menus.” It’s a predictable drill-down from geography to household to family to member. DHS capture is treated as a gated flow, not a casual “start survey” button.

Step 01

Discover

Filter by geography and scan houses/families by HoF and house number.

Step 02

Review family

Family profile: category, housing, facilities, schemes, and update-required cues.

Step 03

Manage members

Add/edit member details, link IDs, record status changes, and prevent accidental deletes.

Step 04

DHS capture

Instruction → presence → house GPS/photo → personal and conditions capture → save.

Routing behavior

  • Work states: “Survey pending” and “Update required” are visible on lists to reduce guesswork.
  • Fast scanning: sort and filter by age, house number, HoF, and family number.
  • DHS gates: presence prompt + house location/photo reduce “survey done but unusable” outcomes.

Design decisions

Designed the register as a field workflow, not a directory

1) Discovery is geography-first

Filters for administrative hierarchy reduce long scrolling and keep the worker anchored to where they are actually operating.

2) “Update required” is a work signal

Update is treated as a state (including “update required since”) to help workers prioritize and supervisors track stale records.

3) Family profile captures determinants, not just demographics

Housing, sanitation, drinking water, cooking fuel, and schemes are structured for consistency because they are often used for program targeting and eligibility.

4) DHS instruction + presence gate prevents premature capture

The flow explicitly asks if the worker is present at the beneficiary’s house and warns what inputs are required (GPS + photo) before entering sensitive data.

5) Destructive actions are guarded

“Delete member” and status changes like “member not alive” are treated as irreversible governance actions and require explicit confirmation.

RBAC as UX

Sensitive edits are role-bound, and the UI makes that obvious

Household and member data is high-impact. The interface treats edit and delete not as convenience actions, but as permissions that must be explained through visibility, confirmations, and constrained entry points.

Boundary 01

View vs edit

Most users review; only authorized roles edit core identity and family baselines.

Boundary 02

DHS capture integrity

Presence + house location/photo gates reduce incomplete surveys and improve auditability.

Boundary 03

Delete + status changes

Delete member and “not alive” require confirmation to prevent accidental data loss.

Edge cases

Built for daily mess: missing IDs, duplicates, and interrupted surveys

No ration card / no Aadhaar flows

The UI supports “Don’t have” paths so capture can proceed without forcing fake entries just to unblock workers.

Linked people / duplicates surfaced

“Linked people” selection reduces duplicate identity creation when a household already exists in the system.

Exit from DHS without damage

Clear “Exit” vs “Continue with DHS” choices reduce accidental abandonment and make intent explicit.

Accidental deletes blocked

“Are you sure you want to delete member from the family list?” confirmation prevents irreversible mistakes under mobile field conditions.

Screens

Key surfaces in the OPD module

These screens illustrate OPD as a routed, multi-role encounter instead of a single clinician form. Each surface is anchored to a “work due” state (vitals, consultation, labs, dispensing) so handoffs are explicit, resumable, and permission-safe across Nurse, MO, Lab Technician, and Pharmacist.

System fit

This is the foundation for longitudinal service delivery

Family Register isn’t a standalone list. It’s how the system anchors households, assigns scope, and prevents repeat capture. When registers are trustworthy, follow-ups and program workflows don’t start from zero each time.

Links to identity + services

Member profiles carry identifiers and health conditions that can feed eligibility, referrals, and continuity flows without redundant questioning.

Governance via “update required”

Update-required states help supervisors maintain data freshness without turning the register into an admin-only system.

Impact

Less duplication, clearer field execution, cleaner capture

  • Reduced duplicate identity creation by surfacing linked people and encouraging selection before new entry.
  • Made field work diagnosable through explicit states (survey pending, update required) rather than hidden errors.
  • Improved DHS capture quality by gating on presence and requiring house GPS + photo before proceeding.
  • Reduced irreversible mistakes with confirmation dialogs for delete and member status changes.

Example counters shown in the UI: Family register shows 55 families and 184 people in a geography slice; families commonly show 3–6 members; update required is tracked with “since 2 months”; DHS completion can surface lightweight progress cues (example: “You have earned 20 Stars”).

What’s next

What’s next: reduce retries without hiding governance

Smarter duplicate resolution

Add clearer “why this match” cues in linked-people selection (name + age + guardian + last update) to reduce incorrect merges and rework.

Offline-first DHS save states

Make save/sync status explicit (saved locally, syncing, failed, retry) so workers trust the system without repeating entries.

Supervisor review surfaces for “update required”

Provide a lightweight queue for stale records and partial surveys so governance becomes scalable rather than reactive.
