MODULE · SAMASTA

Dashboard + admin console for PHC operations at scale

A role-aware dashboard and management console to run PHC deployments: track OPD output and service funnel, monitor attendance, and maintain the operational spine (staff, facilities, and geography). Built for government reality: drill-down by state → district → block → facility, exportable reports, and strict separation between “view stats” and “change master data.”

Problem

Operations fail when “data exists” but accountability doesn’t

In government deployments, the hard part is not generating numbers, it’s making them usable: teams need to know which facilities are active, who showed up, where OPD is rising or collapsing, and whether services are actually reaching patients. Without a governance layer, frontline workflows become isolated, and leadership ends up chasing updates through calls and spreadsheets.

What this module must solve

  • Hierarchical visibility: state → district → block → facility drill-down without rebuilding charts each time.
  • Two modes: analytics + reporting vs master-data management (staff/facility/geo).
  • Exportability: reports must be downloadable for formal review cycles.
  • Exception detection: not just totals, but “who is missing?” and “what is stuck?”
  • Safe admin actions: edits, deactivation, transfer, password reset, migration, with audit-friendly flows.

Users

Designed for governance roles, without punishing facility workflows

This module primarily supports administrative and supervisory roles: they need consistent filters, quick comparisons, and controlled edit capabilities. The UX avoids mixing “monitoring” screens with “doing work” screens.

Program ops / supervisors

Monitors facility performance: OPD trends, service funnel completion (vitals, labs, prescription), attendance health, and report downloads for review meetings.

District/block admins

Maintains staff lists and postings, manages facilities and attributes (HWC, referral, 24×7), and maintains geography (villages, anganwadis) with migration flows.

Information architecture

Two planes: Analytics + Reports vs Master data management

The console is structured around an unavoidable reality: analytics is read-heavy and comparative, while management is edit-heavy and risk-sensitive. Keeping these planes separate reduces accidental edits, permission confusion, and training load.

Plane 01

Analytics + Reports

  • Dashboard trend line (30 days / 6 months / year)
  • OPD overview: new vs revisit vs teleconsultations
  • Service funnel: vitals, labs, prescriptions, diagnosis
  • Breakdowns: age, gender, symptoms, vitals, labs
  • Reports: OPD report with date range + download
  • Attendance dashboards and staff punch-in overview

Plane 02

Master data management

  • Staff: list, view details, create, transfer, deactivate
  • Login controls: generate/reset password, app user status
  • Health facilities: create/edit, service attributes, migrate
  • Geography: district/block/panchayat/village/anganwadi
  • Bulk creation patterns (add more villages/anganwadis)

Design decisions

Designed for drill-down, comparability, and meeting-ready exports

1) Global geo filter spine

The top filter pattern (state, district, block, facility type, facility) acts as a shared language across dashboard, reports, and management. This avoids users having to “re-learn” scoping on every screen.

2) Trend controls that map to review rhythms

Time toggles (30 days, 6 months, year) align to typical operational reviews: daily monitoring, monthly tracking, and annual program reporting. The same control appears consistently across trend surfaces.

3) Service funnel as an operational truth-check

Showing “vitals taken / lab examined / medicine prescribed / diagnosis recorded” makes it hard for the system to hide incomplete clinical workflows behind a single “OPD count.” This is where exceptions get caught.

4) Reports designed for export, not exploration

OPD reports use a date range → table → download flow, because reporting often happens outside the product (reviews, audits, meetings). Exploration stays in the dashboard; reporting stays in reports.

5) Attendance as “who is missing?”

Attendance summary surfaces not just “present %” but the operational buckets: punched in, late, not present, on leave. The detail table supports quick follow-up by designation and facility.

RBAC as UX

Separation of powers: view, export, and edit are not the same permission

In government systems, “who can see” and “who can change” must be explicit. This module treats RBAC as a UI constraint: navigation visibility, action placement, and destructive actions are permission-gated and designed with friction where needed.

Boundary 01

Analytics access

Most supervisory roles can view dashboard metrics and drill-down within their geography scope.

Boundary 02

Export privilege

Download/report actions can be restricted independently to prevent unmanaged sharing of large datasets.

Boundary 03

Master data edits

Staff/facility/geography changes are limited to admins, with “transfer/deactivate/migrate” treated as controlled actions.

Admin workflows

Staff, facilities, and geography as the operational backbone

Staff management

  • Staff list with filters and “App user” status
  • Create staff with personal + posting + login details
  • Transfer staff between postings (PHC/CHC, SC, village)
  • Reset/generate password + send to user
  • Deactivate staff without deleting history

Facilities + geography

  • Facility list with attributes (HWC, referral, MTC, 24×7, Namma Clinic)
  • Create/edit facility and update service details
  • Migration flows for location detail changes
  • Create villages and anganwadis with bulk “Add more” patterns
  • Geography summaries for quick completeness checks

System fit

This module is the governance layer for everything else

OPD, inventory, outreach, and registration workflows succeed only if the system’s backbone is accurate: staff are correctly posted, facilities are correctly typed, and geography mappings are current. This dashboard + admin console ensures the rest of Samasta isn’t forced to “guess the truth.”

Operational continuity

Staff transfer and deactivation flows reduce “ghost users,” and keep app access aligned with real postings.

Reporting reliability

Exportable OPD and attendance reports support review cycles without forcing stakeholders into the product UI.

Impact

Fewer blind spots, faster follow-ups, safer admin operations

  • Geo drill-down makes it easy to compare facilities without building separate dashboards per district.
  • Service funnel views reveal “partial OPD” and missing steps, not just visit totals.
  • Attendance summaries reduce time-to-action for absenteeism and staffing gaps.
  • Admin actions (transfer/deactivate/migrate) make master data maintainable without unsafe deletes.
  • Reports align with government workflows: date range → table → download.

What’s next

Make exceptions actionable, not just visible

Exception queues for governance

Add “needs action” views: facilities with missing staff, low service funnel completion, repeated absenteeism, and incomplete master data (missing mappings, inactive-but-reporting facilities).

Audit-friendly change history

Show “what changed, by whom, when” for staff transfers, facility edits, and geography migrations to support program audits without forensic spreadsheet archaeology.

Report templates for recurring reviews

Support saved report presets (e.g., last month OPD + attendance by district) to speed up recurring meetings and reduce manual setup.
