MODULE · SAMASTA

Outreach activities that don’t collapse into “notes in a diary”

A structured outreach workflow for PHC/SHC teams to plan events, run routine activity types, record participation, and tie health screening outcomes back to follow-ups and referrals. Designed to support real field constraints: fast capture, partial data, and accountability without extra paperwork.

Problem

Outreach becomes invisible when it’s not operationally structured

Outreach is a daily reality in public health: wellness days, screenings, trainings, meetings, and immunization sessions. But teams lose visibility when outreach capture is unstructured, optional, or disconnected from patient follow-up. The result is predictable: activities happen, but the system can’t prove it, plan it, or connect it to care continuity.

What the Outreach module must solve

  • Plan vs run: planned events and routine activities need different capture patterns.
  • Fast field capture: minimal mandatory fields, but still structured enough for reporting.
  • Missed activity visibility: missed is a state, not a shame secret.
  • Screening linkage: screening outcomes must land into follow-up and referral workflows.
  • Operational reporting: “my report today” surfaces work without forcing a dashboard obsession.

Users

Designed for teams who alternate between OPD and community work

Outreach sits alongside OPD and follow-up work. The module supports mixed duty patterns where facility staff move between clinic queues and community activity capture.

PHC: MO / Staff Nurse

Runs planned wellness days, meetings, and screening camps. Needs quick entry, patient search, and clean outcomes (referral/follow-up) without turning the field day into a data-entry internship.

SHC: CHO / ANM

Runs routine activities and targeted screenings. Needs sub-center and village context, plus a simple way to capture participants and evidence (photos) when required.

Village: ASHA (field app)

Feeds beneficiary context and supports mobilization. Outreach capture must remain compatible with fragmented connectivity and partial beneficiary identity.

Admins (dashboard)

Needs aggregated views: planned vs conducted counts, missed activities, and screening outputs by geography and facility.

Flow

Outreach modeled as a daily surface: planned events + routine activity entry

The module starts with “Today” because that’s how field work actually happens. From there, users can move into planned event execution, routine activity creation, or health screening flows that generate follow-up work.

Key states and transitions

  • Planned → Conducted: the event becomes an execution record with outcomes.
  • Planned → Missed: missed activity remains visible for governance and planning.
  • Screening → Follow-up due: screening can generate follow-up tasks with dates and reasons.

Design decisions

Designed for high variance, low time, and inconsistent connectivity

1) “Routine Activities” as shortcuts, not a menu maze

Routine activities are presented as a compact set of entry points (wellness activity, health screening, training, meeting) so users can start capture in under a minute.

2) Event lists optimized for scanning

Upcoming, previous, and missed events use the same card pattern with date/time and activity type, so the user can spot what needs attention without reading paragraphs.

3) “Participants + photos” are treated as evidence, not decoration

Capture supports participant count and a limited photo upload, giving program teams a basic audit trail without bloating the workflow.

4) Screening flows reuse familiar patterns

Health screenings reuse the same patient search and follow-up/referral patterns used elsewhere, reducing training load across modules and roles.

5) “My report today” keeps reporting lightweight

A simple daily report view surfaces counts and activity summaries without forcing users into dashboards during service delivery.

RBAC as UX

Permissions define who can record, verify, and report outreach

Outreach activity capture affects reporting and program accountability, so the UI treats permissions as first-class: users see only the activity types and actions that match their facility role and geography scope.

Boundary 01

Create activity

Facility roles can create routine activities and execute planned events within their geography scope.

Boundary 02

Run screening

Screening actions are scoped to authorized users, with follow-up/referral output flowing to clinical roles.

Boundary 03

Aggregate reporting

Admin views prioritize visibility and exceptions, not operational edits to past records.

Edge cases

Built for missed days, partial info, and duplicate identity

Missed activity as a visible state

Missed events remain on the timeline so teams can follow up and program ops can understand gaps without hunting in WhatsApp threads.

Patient search with ambiguity

Search supports name / mobile / ID patterns, with sub-center and village filters to reduce false matches in high-similarity populations.

“No events found” empty states

Date-based views provide explicit empty states so users don’t confuse “no data” with “sync failed.”

Photo limits for low bandwidth

Photo uploads are capped to keep capture feasible in rural network conditions and avoid blocking “Save” on heavy media.

Screens

Key surfaces in the Outreach module

These screens show outreach as an operational surface, not free-form diary notes. The flow separates planned events from routine activities, keeps “missed” as an explicit state, and turns screening outcomes into follow-up or referral work. Capture stays lightweight: minimal fields, participant counts, and limited photo evidence for audit without slowing field teams.

System fit

Outreach connects planning, delivery, and continuity

Outreach matters only if outcomes land somewhere. The module is designed to connect community activity capture to follow-ups, referrals, and facility workflows so care doesn’t reset every time a patient shows up again.

Follow-up generation

Screening can generate “follow-up due” tasks with dates, keeping outreach outcomes actionable instead of archived.

Program ops visibility

Planned vs conducted vs missed surfaces patterns at facility and geography level without demanding extra reporting work.

Impact

More completed activities, fewer missing outcomes, less “trust me bro” reporting

  • Daily “Today” surface reduces the effort required to start and complete outreach capture.
  • Missed states stay visible, enabling rescheduling and realistic planning.
  • Routine activities become comparable across facilities because activity types are standardized.
  • Screening outcomes feed follow-up and referral workflows instead of disappearing into notes.

What’s next

Tighten operational control without making capture heavier

Exception views for missed and pending outcomes

Add exception lists: planned events without completion, screenings without follow-up/referral decision, and activities missing participants/photos where required.

Offline-safe capture

Make routine activity capture fully offline-first with conflict-safe sync so field work doesn’t stop when networks do.

Smarter planning support

Use historical attendance and screening output patterns to suggest activity timing and village targeting, without turning it into “AI suggestions” no one asked for.
