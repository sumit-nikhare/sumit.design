Overview

Samasta is a two-surface platform: a field application built for task execution under real-world constraints, and an operations dashboard built for supervision, backlog management, and governance across administrative levels.

Field application

  • Work-queue based navigation (today, overdue, high-priority)
  • Offline-tolerant capture with explicit sync states
  • Consistent entity headers for people/households/records

Operations dashboard

  • Backlogs, exceptions, and assignment controls
  • Geographic drill-down from state to facility/worker
  • Data quality and timeliness visibility (not just charts)

This page acts as the Samasta case study index. Each module is documented separately to keep the system narrative readable.

Problem

As the platform expanded across programs, geographies, and partner organizations, the UX began to fail in predictable ways: role overload for frontline users, inconsistent patterns across modules, and RBAC implemented as backend logic rather than a UI constraint.

UI and RBAC didn’t scale

Permissions existed, but screens were not designed around them.

Inconsistent behaviors across modules

Same task, different flows, higher errors and retraining burden.

High cognitive load in the field

Frontline roles saw too many options and too much information.

Low-connectivity realities

Offline states were unclear, sync failures were opaque, and trust suffered.

Approach

I treated Samasta as a modular platform rather than a set of screens. The work focused on shared patterns, permission-aware navigation, and operational flows that survive real constraints.

1

Define platform primitives

Entity headers, worklists, status language, drill-down patterns.

2

Bake RBAC into UI structure

Visibility, action affordances, geography scoping, auditability.

3

Design for operations

Backlogs, exceptions, assignment, and data quality as tasks.

Core constraints

  • Multi-role usage across administrative levels
  • Training and adoption in distributed teams
  • Low-end devices and unstable connectivity
  • Governance requirements: audit trails, approval flows, and controlled access

RBAC foundations

RBAC was treated as a navigation and interaction problem, not a backend filter. Role and geography drive: what appears, what is actionable, and how drill-down works.

Visibility rules

  • Hide entire modules that are irrelevant to a role
  • Show read-only views when oversight is required
  • Explain disabled actions with the “why” (reduces support churn)

Geography scoping

  • Every list, chart, and export is scope-aware
  • Drill-down respects assigned administrative boundary
  • Assignment actions are constrained to jurisdiction

Role families (illustrative)

Field worker

Execute tasks, capture visits, handle offline/sync

Supervisor

Assign, verify, resolve exceptions, monitor sync gaps

Facility / program ops

Referrals, readiness, follow-ups, compliance

Outcomes

The work improved consistency across modules, reduced role overload by aligning UI to permissions, and made operational workflows explicit in the dashboard (backlogs, exceptions, assignment), instead of burying them behind summary-only reporting.

Consistency

Shared patterns for lists, headers, statuses, drill-down.

Role clarity

Less noise for field roles, clearer read-only oversight.

Operational control

Supervisors can act on exceptions without exports.

Trust under low connectivity

Explicit offline/sync states reduce ambiguity and rework.
