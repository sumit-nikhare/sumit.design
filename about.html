About me

I’m a Product Designer with 3.8+ years of experience shaping end-to-end digital experiences across healthtech, public sector platforms, and B2B ecosystems. My work blends user research, systems thinking, and clean visual design to build products that are not only intuitive but meaningful at scale.

I’ve designed solutions for large government programs, community healthcare workers, and enterprise stakeholders, giving me strong experience working with complex workflows, technical constraints, and diverse user groups. Whether it’s simplifying a multi-step medical workflow or designing tools for channel partners and field teams, I focus on creating clarity, reducing effort, and driving measurable impact.

My approach is grounded in evidence over assumptions—I rely on field observations, user interviews, and data to inform decisions. I enjoy collaborating closely with PMs, engineers, content designers, and data teams to ensure every design holds up through implementation and delivers value in the real world.

My Design Principles

Good principles have sacrifices. They prioritize certain benefits over others, build constraints into one’s workflow, and act as a guide toward performance and efficiency.

Here are four principles that guide my product design and team leadership.

Design is 90% Communication

Communication is the most important skillset in product design. This includes team facilitation, persuasive storytelling, and stakeholder management.

Infinite Mindset

Adopting a purpose-driven approach and playing for the long term is more effective than short-term approaches like “beating the competition”.

Outcomes Over Output

Customer behavior is the most important metric for business success. Solving problems begins by prioritizing outcomes, not features.

80% Is Good Enough

Reaching 80% of a goal is usually enough. The time to get to 100% satisfaction is often better spent making progress elsewhere.
