About me

I’m a product designer, mentor, and growth strategist who helps mission-driven teams turn insights into digital experiences that convert, retain, and scale.

I studied PR and advertising (yes, in the film school), then launched my career in boutique marketing and event agencies working on custom campaigns for brands. I wore many hats: copywriter, graphic designer, social media strategist, deck builder, and even event producer.

Eventually the grind wore thin, and I pivoted into UX. These days, I work independently with startups and small businesses, bringing a mix of craft, strategy, and growth thinking to each project.

Outside of work, I’m a certified arts & crafts nerd. You’ll often find me deep in a coloring book. I also take my playlist curation very seriously. If you work with me, expect thoughtful questions, intentional design, and the occasional surprise cameo.

My Design Principles

Good principles have sacrifices. They prioritize certain benefits over others, build constraints into one’s workflow, and act as a guide toward performance and efficiency.

Here are four principles that guide my product design and team leadership.

Design is 90% Communication

Communication is the most important skillset in product design. This includes team facilitation, persuasive storytelling, and stakeholder management.

Infinite Mindset

Adopting a purpose-driven approach and playing for the long term is more effective than short-term approaches like “beating the competition”.

Outcomes Over Output

Customer behavior is the most important metric for business success. Solving problems begins by prioritizing outcomes, not features.

80% Is Good Enough

Reaching 80% of a goal is usually enough. The time to get to 100% satisfaction is often better spent making progress elsewhere.

Roles & Responsibilities

In my current role, I lead end-to-end product design responsibilities—from framing problems with cross-functional partners to delivering validated, implementation-ready solutions. I work closely with PMs, engineers, data teams, and field staff to ensure every design decision is grounded in real user needs and measurable outcomes.

Discover the Problem

  1. Partner with Product to frame the problem using evidence, research, and data—not assumptions.
  2. Audit current workflows, friction points, competitors, and adjacent patterns.
  3. Define research questions, methods, and participant criteria.
  4. Collaborate on metric definitions to ensure measurable success.
  5. Document assumptions, risks, and a validation plan.

Define the Direction

  1. Synthesize research into insights, JTBD, needs, and opportunity areas.
  2. Establish experience principles and guardrails (accessibility, privacy, constraints).
  3. Draft high-level flows and IA; align on MVP vs. future scopes.
  4. Co-author user stories and acceptance criteria with PM/Engineering.
  5. Create instrumentation plans with Data teams.

Design the Solution

  • Develop low-fidelity explorations and run quick validation checks.
  • Create annotated high-fidelity designs using tokens and components.
  • Define micro-interactions, edge cases, errors, and empty states.
  • Collaborate with Content Design on UI text and tone.
  • Ensure WCAG 2.2 AA compliance with test notes.

Validate with Users

  • Plan and run usability tests; gather and synthesize findings.
  • Iterate and document decisions, rationale, and trade-offs.
  • Conduct pre-mortem to assess risks and adjust scope.
  • Complete accessibility, security, and privacy reviews.

Partner Through Build

  • Deliver complete specs—flows, redlines, tokens, assets, content matrix.
  • Run engineering walkthroughs and maintain a shared decision log.
  • Review dev/staging builds; file actionable issues.
  • Validate analytics events and data contracts.

Prepare for Launch

  • Perform final design QA on release candidates.
  • Prepare screenshots, release notes, and help content.
  • Align on experiment parameters and guardrails.
  • Provide go/no-go input during release reviews.

Measure Post Launch

  • Monitor dashboards and verify no regressions.
  • Lead experiment readouts; propose next iteration.
  • Capture learnings and update the design system when needed.
  • Close the loop with support and marketing on outcomes.

Beyond delivery, I continuously collaborate with teams to monitor impact, refine workflows, and evolve our design system. My goal is to ensure the product not only ships smoothly but continues to improve through evidence, feedback, and iteration.
