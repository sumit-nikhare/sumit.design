DESIGN PRINCIPLES

My Design Principles

Good principles have sacrifices. They prioritize certain benefits over others, build constraints into one’s workflow, and act as a guide toward performance and efficiency.

Here are four principles that guide my product design and team leadership.

Design is 90% Communication

Communication is the most important skillset in product design. This includes team facilitation, persuasive storytelling, and stakeholder management.

Infinite Mindset

Adopting a purpose-driven approach and playing for the long term is more effective than short-term approaches like “beating the competition”.

Outcomes Over Output

Customer behavior is the most important metric for business success. Solving problems begins by prioritizing outcomes, not features.

80% Is Good Enough

Reaching 80% of a goal is usually enough. The time to get to 100% satisfaction is often better spent making progress elsewhere.

SERVICES

Services

Design support for public-sector and health platforms, focused on role-based workflows, operational constraints, and scalable systems.

Role-based Workflow Design (RBAC-aware UX)

I design end-to-end flows where permissions are visible in the interface: scoped navigation, role-aware actions, and safe defaults. This reduces clutter for frontline users, prevents backend-only RBAC surprises, and keeps accountability clear across facilities and geographies.

Complex Forms & Task Flow Simplification

I turn long, error-prone workflows into resumable steps with better defaults, field-real validations, and clear recovery states. This reduces mistakes, speeds completion, and prevents users getting stuck when connectivity drops or information is incomplete.

Offline-first & Low-connectivity UX

I design for intermittent networks: clear sync states, conflict-safe editing, local drafts, and predictable retries. Users can keep working on low-end devices, understand what’s saved, and avoid duplicate submissions when signal disappears mid-task.

Design Systems for Modular Platforms

I build tokens, components, and reusable patterns that scale across modules and teams, including role-aware tables, forms, filters, and status messaging. This reduces redesign and dev rework, keeps experiences consistent, and makes governance changes cheaper to roll out.

Research, Validation & Field Feedback Loops

I run lightweight, decision-focused research through field visits, remote checks, and rapid synthesis into actionable changes. Feedback loops are structured around what can ship, helping teams validate assumptions, reduce risk, and improve continuously without months of process.
